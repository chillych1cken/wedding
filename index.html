Day 1: Mehendi, Pani puri and Ros

22nd of April
Afternoon Open End (TBC)

Starting of the festivities with a colourful and intimate mehendi ceremony for the bride and guests (and the groom if he wants to partake). We’re gonna have unlimited Pani Puri, snacks, music and games!

Our Goan Ros ceremony is traditionally held in the late evening. We will have some tribal kunbi dancers and a band. The bridal party is “anointed” with coconut oil and milk and eggs after which things start to get ..creative. The event will finish with a dinner and end with a typical Goan mando session (usually led by Nicoles father, Tomas)

Travel Advice - Weather

The weather conditions are hot and humid, so please take light, breathable clothing and also mosquito repellent. Please avoid heavy dresses, layers, jackets, coats. We want you to enjoy and be sweatless.

Travel Advice - Flight Routes & Airlines

  • Qatar Airways: Major cities in Europe (London, Paris, Frankfurt, etc.) to Doha, then onward to Goa, Dabolim.
  • Emirates: Major European cities (London, Manchester, Paris, Frankfurt, etc.) to Dubai, then onward to Goa, Dabolim Airport.
  • Etihad Airways: European cities (London, Paris, Frankfurt) to Abu Dhabi, then onward to Goa.
  • Lufthansa: European cities (Frankfurt, Munich) to Mumbai or Delhi, connecting to Goa via Air India or other domestic carriers.
  • Turkish Airlines: Major European cities (London, Paris, Amsterdam, Frankfurt) to Istanbul, connecting to Mumbai or Delhi, and then to Goa, Dabolim Airport.

Key Hubs for Transfers

  • Mumbai (BOM): Closest major international airport to Goa with frequent domestic flights.
  • Delhi (DEL): Another major hub with numerous connecting flights to Goa.
  • Doha (DOH), Dubai (DXB), Abu Dhabi (AUH): Key layover points for travelers from Europe to Goa.

From Indian hubs like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad, domestic airlines like IndiGo, SpiceJet, AirAsia India, and Vistara offer multiple daily flights to Goa, usually lasting 1-2 hours.
